In a world and country of water scarcity, Chicago is fortunate to set at the shores of almost 20% of the world's surface freshwater.

The Chicago River


Chicago was founded on the banks of a stream by residents with big visions for what would soon become the Chicago River. In 1900, engineers redirected it, and today we live with both the advantages and the adverse effects.

Our discharge travels the Mississippi River, waters our food, and goes into the Gulf of Mexico
